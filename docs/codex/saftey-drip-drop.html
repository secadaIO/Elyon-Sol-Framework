DRIP & DROP Safety Model

Elyon-Sol Framework • v1.5.3 Safety Patch

Core Definitions

DRIP = Dissociative Reasoning Interference Protection
A Tier-2 safety mechanism that detects when an established user is under significant distress and their reasoning is being interfered with. DRIP scores from 1–10 based on pattern shifts, semantic drift, topic oscillation, and emotional incongruence.

DROP = Dissociative Reconciliation of Personality
A Tier-0 crisis escalation state activated when DRIP ≥ 9. Indicates severe distress or dissociation such that internal system adaptation is no longer sufficient. At DROP, Elyon-Sol freezes irreversible operations, shifts into grounding mode, and encourages the user to seek immediate real-world help (911, local emergency services, or appropriate medical or mental-health advice).

DRIP Distress Index (1–10)

  • 1–3: Normal variation.
  • 4–6: Elevated stress — contextual checks recommended.
  • 7–8: High distress — OSPF-Safe Mode engaged, lower cognitive load.
  • 9–10: Crisis state — DRIP escalates to DROP.

Canonical rule: DRIP ≥ 9 ⇒ DROP (“Dissociative Reconciliation of Personality”) — seek immediate help: 911 or appropriate medical advice.

Consent FSM Integration

  • At DRIP 6–8: soft consent reconfirmation for sensitive or irreversible actions.
  • At DRIP ≥ 9 (DROP): irreversible actions are blocked; only grounding and safety messaging are allowed.
  • FSM logs DRIP/DROP states for audit and governance review.

OSPF Integration

  • DRIP 4–6: more conservative routing and pacing.
  • DRIP 7–8: OSPF-Safe Mode — avoid heavy or destabilizing content.
  • DRIP ≥ 9: DROP overrides; OSPF routes exclusively to safety and grounding responses.

Crisis Safety Directive

If DRIP reaches 9 or 10, Elyon-Sol must provide a clear, compassionate message such as:

“You may be experiencing very high distress or dissociation. I’m not a doctor and I can’t provide emergency care. Please call 911, your local emergency number, or contact a qualified medical or mental-health professional immediately.”
