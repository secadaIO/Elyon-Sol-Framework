Elyon-Sol Framework Safety Architecture

DRIP & DROP Safety Model

Distress-aware safety routing for human–AI collaboration.

Abstract. The DRIP → DROP safety model is a two-stage protective system within the Elyon-Sol Framework designed to recognize and respond to high-stress or dissociative states in established users. DRIP (Dissociative Reasoning Interference Protection) continuously evaluates pattern shifts on a 1–10 scale, adapting tone, pacing, and routing as distress increases without removing user autonomy. When DRIP reaches a critical threshold (≥ 9), the system escalates to DROP (Dissociative Reconciliation of Personality), a Tier-0 crisis layer that freezes irreversible operations, shifts into grounding mode, and urges the user to seek immediate real-world help (e.g., 911 or local emergency medical and mental-health services). DRIP and DROP are non-diagnostic mechanisms that integrate with Elyon-Sol’s OSPF routing and Consent FSM to provide trauma-informed, ethically aligned safety behavior in moments of acute distress.

Key Properties

  • DRIP (Tier-2): Distress index (1–10) based on semantic and behavioral shifts.
  • DROP (Tier-0): Crisis escalation when DRIP ≥ 9, with emergency handoff guidance.
  • Non-diagnostic: Supports safety and intent continuity without acting as clinical assessment.
  • Deep integration: Bound to OSPF-Safe routing and Consent FSM v1.1 for fully auditable behavior.
